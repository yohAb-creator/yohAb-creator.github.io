    About Me

    May 1, 2025 • 1 min read • Yohannes • — views

    Hi, I’m Yohannes

    Physicist and mathematician focused on computational modeling and topological methods. I study how structure arises from data—whether in the geometry of high-dimensional embeddings or the dynamics of physical systems. My work bridges theory and implementation, combining machine learning and algebraic topology to build models that explain and predict.

    Currently, I’m exploring how topological tools can extract generalized reasoning pathways from and for Large Language Models.

    What I’m working on

    • Topology for LLMs: extracting stable “reasoning shapes” inside model activations.
    • Geometric ML: using manifold-based priors to keep models faithful to structure.
    • Scientific tooling: building lean pipelines for experiments, visualization, and reproducibility.

    Education

    • BA in Mathematics and Physics — College of Wooster (2024)

    Interests

    • Algebraic Topology · Algebraic Geometry · Topological Data Analysis
    • Machine Learning · Differential Geometry · Advanced Computational Methods

    Selected highlights

    • Topological Prompt Distillation: using persistent homology to prune prompts while preserving downstream accuracy.
    • TDA-augmented competitive programming: exploring topology as a lens on algorithm behavior and generalization.
    • Geometry-first visualizations: building interactive demos that explain complex models with minimal friction.

    Let’s collaborate

    I enjoy pairing with researchers and engineers on experiments, prototypes, and visualization-heavy writeups. If you have an idea worth testing, let’s make it tangible.
